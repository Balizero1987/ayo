## DIVISION 58: PUBLISHING ACTIVITIES

### KBLI 5811 - Book Publishing

**Full Description:**
- **Indonesian:** Penerbitan Buku (Book Publishing)
- **English:** Book publishing business including print, electronic (CD, CD-ROM, DVD), audio or internet formats

**Foreign Ownership:** CLOSED to foreign investment
- Book publishing is allocated for small and middle-size companies only
- Big scale business including foreign investment is NOT allowed
- Foreign parties must cooperate with Indonesian-owned publishing companies

**Investment Minimum:** IDR 10 billion (USD ~660,000) minimum for PT PMA structure (if cooperation structure used)

**Risk Level:** Medium-Low (MR) - Requires NIB + Self-Declaration Certificate

**Required Licenses:**
- NIB (Business Identification Number) via OSS
- Certificate of Standard (self-declared)
- ISBN registration through National Library of Indonesia (Perpusnas)
- Publisher registration with relevant authorities

**Mandatory Certifications:**
- ISBN (International Standard Book Number) - mandatory for all published books
- Application through: https://isbn.perpusnas.go.id/
- Publisher must register as legitimate publishing entity

**Timeline to Operations:**
- Company setup: 9-10 weeks
- NIB issuance: 1 day after OSS registration
- ISBN registration: 1-2 weeks
- Total estimated: 12-14 weeks

**Cost Estimates:**
- PT setup: USD 3,000 - 3,500
- Notary fees: USD 50 - 100
- OSS registration: USD 100 - 300
- ISBN registration: Contact Perpusnas (fees vary)
- Virtual office (if needed): USD 430/year
- **Total startup costs:** USD 4,000 - 5,000

**Common Mistakes & Warnings:**
- CRITICAL: Foreign investors cannot own book publishing companies directly
- Must establish cooperation structure with Indonesian partners
- ISBN registration is MANDATORY - cannot publish without it
- Copyright agreements must be properly documented with authors
- Must provide accurate sales data to copyright holders
- Violating foreign ownership rules can result in license revocation

**Legal Framework:**
- Law No. 3/2017 on Book System
- Government Regulation No. 75/2019
- Perpres 10/2021 Positive Investment List

**Related KBLI Codes:**
- 5812: Publishing of directories and mailing lists
- 5813: Publishing of newspapers, journals, periodicals
- 5820: Software publishing
- 63122: Web portal/digital platform for content

## DIVISION 59: MOTION PICTURE, VIDEO, AND TELEVISION PRODUCTION

### KBLI 5911 - Motion Picture, Video, and TV Program Production

**Sub-classifications:**
- **59111:** Government-operated production
- **59112:** Private sector production (most common for foreign investors)

**Full Description:**
- **Indonesian:** Aktivitas Produksi Film, Video dan Program Televisi
- **English:** Production of motion pictures, films, videos, animation, television programs, television advertising; includes film production for television and film delivery services

**Foreign Ownership:** 100% OPEN (since 2016 liberalization)
- Presidential Regulation No. 44/2016 opened film production to 100% foreign investment
- Major liberalization from previous complete closure

**Investment Minimum:** IDR 10 billion (USD ~660,000) excluding land and buildings

**Risk Level:** Medium (M) to Medium-High (MT) depending on scope

**Required Licenses:**
- NIB via OSS system
- Certificate of Standard (verified for medium-high risk)
- LSF (Lembaga Sensor Film) approval for all content
- Film permit for foreign productions
- Location permits from local authorities
- Ministry of Education and Culture approval

**Mandatory Certifications:**
- **LSF Certificate (STLS - Surat Tanda Lulus Sensor)** - MANDATORY
  - Required before any public distribution
  - Processing time: 4 working days (standard service)
  - Assessment covers theme, dialog, visual, subtitle
  - Cannot distribute without LSF approval
- Foreign production work permits (C14 visa for crew)

**Timeline to Operations:**
- PT PMA setup: 9-10 weeks
- NIB: 1 day
- Certificate of Standard verification: 2-4 weeks
- LSF approval per content: 4 working days
- Film permit (for foreign productions): 4-6 weeks
- **Total for company setup: 12-16 weeks**
- **Per-production permits: 4-6 weeks**

**Cost Estimates:**
- PT PMA setup: USD 3,000 - 3,500
- Minimum capital: USD 635,000 (paid-up)
- LSF submission fees: Contact LSF (lsf.go.id)
- Film permits: Varies by production scale
- **Total startup: USD 5,000 - 7,000 (excluding capital)**

**Common Mistakes & Warnings:**
- CRITICAL: All content MUST receive LSF approval before distribution
- Foreign productions MUST work with locally registered company
- Content violations can result in severe penalties
- Indonesian content quotas apply for broadcasters (60% Indonesian content)
- Film permits require detailed documentation (synopsis, locations, crew list)
- Equipment carnet required for temporary import of filming equipment
- Thursday clearinghouse meetings determine permit approvals
- Processing delays common if documentation incomplete

**Legal Framework:**
- Law No. 33/2009 on Film
- Perpres 44/2016 (100% foreign ownership allowed)
- Perpres 10/2021 (confirmed openness)
- Ministry of Education and Culture Regulation No. 11/2021

**Related KBLI Codes:**
- 5912: Post-production services
- 5913: Motion picture distribution
- 5914: Motion picture projection (cinemas)
- 5920: Sound recording
- 60202: Television broadcasting

## DIVISION 60: PROGRAMMING AND BROADCASTING ACTIVITIES

### KBLI 6010 - Radio Broadcasting

**Sub-classifications:**
- **60101:** Government/public radio
- **60102:** Private/commercial radio (most relevant for investors)

**Full Description:**
- **Indonesian:** Penyiaran Radio
- **English:** Radio broadcasting activities including content creation, acquisition, and broadcasting of radio programs to the public

**Foreign Ownership:** SEVERELY RESTRICTED
- **New private radio stations (KBLI 60102): CLOSED to foreign investment (0%)**
- **Existing private radio stations: Maximum 20% foreign ownership**
  - Only for business expansion/capital raising
  - Must be established by local entity first
  - Requires trial period and permanent license completion

**Investment Minimum:** IDR 10 billion (USD ~660,000)

**Risk Level:** High (T) - Highest risk classification

**Required Licenses:**
- NIB via OSS system
- **Broadcasting Operational License (Izin Penyelenggaraan Penyiaran/IPP)**
  - Application through MCIT online system
  - Assessment by Directorate General of Post and Informatics
  - Assessment by KPI (Indonesian Broadcasting Commission)
  - MCIT issues final approval/rejection
- Frequency allocation from MCIT
- Certificate of Standard (verified)
- Location permits
- Technical infrastructure approval

**Mandatory Certifications:**
- Broadcasting license from Ministry of Communication and Information Technology (MCIT)
- KPI (Komisi Penyiaran Indonesia) content approval
- Frequency license from MCIT
- Technical equipment certification
- Studio safety certification

**Timeline to Operations:**
- PT PMDN (local company) setup: 6-8 weeks
- NIB: 1 day
- Broadcasting operational license: **MINIMUM 6 MONTHS** from complete submission
- Frequency allocation: 2-4 months
- Technical setup: 2-3 months
- **Total: 12-18 MONTHS**

**License Validity:**
- Broadcasting operational license: **5 YEARS**
- Renewable upon expiration

**Cost Estimates:**
- PT PMDN setup (local): USD 1,500 - 2,500
- Broadcasting license fees: Contact MCIT (varies)
- Frequency fees: Annual fees apply
- Studio equipment: USD 50,000 - 200,000+
- Transmitter equipment: USD 100,000 - 500,000+
- **Total investment: USD 200,000 - 1,000,000+**

**Operational Requirements:**
- **Domestic content: MINIMUM 60% of daily broadcast must be Indonesian content**
- Foreign program relay: Maximum 5-10% of daily broadcast
- Advertising: Maximum 20% of daily broadcast for commercial advertising
- Social community service advertising: Minimum 10% must be allocated
- Cannot relay foreign news programs
- Prohibited content: Inappropriate music, violent sports, content against national values

**Common Mistakes & Warnings:**
- CRITICAL: Foreign investors CANNOT establish new radio stations in Indonesia
- 20% rule only applies to EXISTING stations for expansion
- Application process extremely lengthy (6+ months)
- KPI has broad authority to impose sanctions including license revocation
- Content violations result in:
  - Written reprimands
  - Temporary program cessation
  - Broadcast duration limitations
  - Administrative fines
  - License revocation (severe cases)
- Must have at least 2 shareholders for 20% foreign investment scenario
- 60% Indonesian content quota strictly enforced
- Cannot start operations without operational license (illegal broadcasting = criminal offense)
- Frequency allocation highly competitive and limited

**Regulatory Oversight:**
- **MCIT (Ministry of Communication and Information Technology):** Licensing authority
- **KPI (Komisi Penyiaran Indonesia):** Content oversight, independent state institution
- **Directorate of Broadcasting:** Technical assessment
- **Regional KPI:** Regional content monitoring

**Legal Framework:**
- Broadcasting Law
- Perpres 10/2021 (20% foreign ownership cap for expansion)
- MCIT regulations on broadcasting
- KPI content regulations

**Related KBLI Codes:**
- 6020: Television broadcasting
- 5911: Program production
- 5920: Sound recording

## GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

### PT PMA (Foreign-Owned Company) Requirements

**Minimum Capital Requirements:**
- **Authorized capital:** No specific minimum
- **Issued and paid-up capital:** Minimum IDR 10 billion (USD ~660,000)
- **Paid-up capital deposit:** Typically 25% at incorporation (IDR 2.5 billion minimum)
- **Investment plan:** Minimum IDR 10 billion per 5-digit KBLI per project (excluding land and buildings)

**Shareholder Requirements:**
- Minimum 2 shareholders
- Can be individual or corporate entities
- Foreign shareholders allowed per ownership limits
- Director and Commissioner required (can be same nationality as shareholders)

**Registration Timeline:**
| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 1-2 | Business activity confirmation, documentation preparation |
| 3 | Deed of Establishment drafting and notarization |
| 4-5 | Ministry of Law and Human Rights approval |
| 6 | NPWP (Tax ID) and NIB application via OSS |
| 7-8 | Sector-specific business licenses |
| 9-10 | Office lease, bank account opening, capital deposit |

**Standard Documents Required:**
- Passport copies of shareholders/directors
- Proof of address
- Bank reference letters
- Company name reservation
- Articles of Association
- Deed of Establishment (notarized)
- Domicile letter
- Office lease agreement

### OSS (Online Single Submission) System

**NIB (Business Identification Number):**
- Issued within 1 day of registration
- Serves as:
  - Company registration certificate
  - Import identification number (API)
  - Customs access
  - Business license foundation

**Risk-Based Licensing:**

| Risk Level | Indonesian | Requirements |
|------------|-----------|--------------|
| Low | Rendah (R) | NIB only |
| Medium-Low | Menengah Rendah (MR) | NIB + Self-declared Certificate of Standard |
| Medium-High | Menengah Tinggi (MT) | NIB + Verified Certificate of Standard |
| High | Tinggi (T) | NIB + Verified Certificate + Operating License (IZIN) |

**Certificate of Standard:**
- Self-declared: Same day issuance
- Verified: 2-4 weeks for government verification
- Required commitments:
  - Compliance with business standards
  - Environmental management (UKL-UPL)
  - Labor regulations
  - Health and safety standards

### Standard Operating Costs

**Annual Compliance Costs:**

## COMMON MISTAKES & CRITICAL WARNINGS

### Top 10 Critical Mistakes Foreign Investors Make

**1. Misunderstanding Foreign Ownership Restrictions**
- MISTAKE: Assuming all media/publishing businesses allow foreign investment
- REALITY:
  - Book publishing (5811): CLOSED
  - Newspapers (5813): CLOSED for private companies, 49% for public companies only
  - New broadcasting (60102, 60202): CLOSED
  - Only existing broadcast entities can have 20% foreign investment
- CONSEQUENCE: License rejection, forced divestiture, legal penalties

**2. Confusing KBLI Classifications**
- MISTAKE: Choosing wrong KBLI code (e.g., 5820 for software development instead of 6201)
- REALITY: Each 5-digit code has specific activities, ownership rules, and risk levels
- CONSEQUENCE: License rejection, inability to conduct planned activities, forced re-registration

**3. Underestimating Timeline Requirements**
- MISTAKE: Expecting 4-6 week company setup for broadcasting business
- REALITY:
  - Radio broadcasting license: 6+ months
  - Television broadcasting license: 1+ year
  - Cinema operation: 6-9 months
- CONSEQUENCE: Cash flow problems, missed business opportunities, investor relations issues

**4. Ignoring Content Requirements**
- MISTAKE: Planning 100% foreign content for broadcasting
- REALITY:
  - 60% Indonesian content MANDATORY for radio and TV
  - Cannot relay foreign news
  - LSF approval required for all film/video
- CONSEQUENCE: License suspension, heavy fines, criminal charges

**5. Insufficient Capital Planning**
- MISTAKE: Preparing only IDR 10 billion for television station
- REALITY: Television requires IDR 150-750+ billion (USD 10-50 million+)
- CONSEQUENCE: Project failure, inability to meet licensing requirements

**6. Neglecting LSF Requirements**
- MISTAKE: Distributing content without LSF approval
- REALITY: ALL film/video content for public distribution requires LSF certificate
- CONSEQUENCE: Distribution ban, heavy fines, content seizure, criminal charges

**7. Missing Mandatory Certifications**
- MISTAKE: Operating cinema without 60% Indonesian content
- REALITY: Quarterly reporting required, strict enforcement
- CONSEQUENCE: License revocation, business closure

**8. Improper Corporate Structure**
- MISTAKE: Setting up PT PMA for book publishing as sole foreign investor
- REALITY: Book publishing requires Indonesian ownership
- CONSEQUENCE: License denial, wasted setup costs

**9. Inadequate Regulatory Understanding**
- MISTAKE: Not understanding KPI's authority over broadcasting
- REALITY: KPI can immediately suspend broadcasts for content violations
- CONSEQUENCE: Business interruption, revenue loss, reputation damage

**10. Failing to Maintain Compliance**
- MISTAKE: Not filing quarterly reports, not maintaining screening logs
- REALITY: Regular reporting mandatory, inspections common
- CONSEQUENCE: Administrative fines, license suspension, revocation

- Television broadcasting (6020): CLOSED for new, 20% max for existing

- Broadcasting (radio/TV): 6-12+ month licensing, massive capital requirements, 60% content quota
