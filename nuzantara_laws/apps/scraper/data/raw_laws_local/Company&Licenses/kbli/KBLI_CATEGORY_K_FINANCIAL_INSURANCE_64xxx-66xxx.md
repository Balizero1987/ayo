# KBLI Category K: Financial and Insurance Activities (64xxx-66xxx)

**COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE TO INDONESIAN STANDARD INDUSTRIAL CLASSIFICATION**
**Category K - Financial Intermediation, Insurance, and Auxiliary Financial Services**

---

## ⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING: HIGHLY REGULATED SECTOR

**This is Indonesia's MOST HEAVILY REGULATED business sector. Key facts:**

- **OJK (Otoritas Jasa Keuangan) approval is MANDATORY** for all financial services
- **Minimum capital requirements: IDR 50 billion to IDR 3 TRILLION** depending on activity
- **Timeline to operations: 12-24 months** (vs. 1-3 months for other sectors)
- **Foreign ownership: Heavily restricted** - Banking limited to 40%, Insurance to 80%
- **Licensing complexity: Multi-stage process** with fit & proper tests, capital adequacy reviews
- **NO "test and learn" approach** - You must be fully capitalized and compliant BEFORE starting

**DO NOT attempt to establish a financial services company without expert legal counsel and substantial capital backing.**

## DIVISION 641: MONETARY INTERMEDIATION (BANKING)

### KBLI 64110 - Central Banking (Bank Sentral)

**Indonesian:** Perbankan Sentral
**English:** Central Banking

#### Description
Activities including issuing and managing the country's currency, monitoring and controlling the money supply, taking deposits used for clearing between financial institutions, holding international reserves, and acting as banker for the government. This specifically covers **Bank Indonesia's** activities as the state institution functioning as the Central Bank.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:** 0% - **CLOSED TO FOREIGN INVESTMENT** (State monopoly)
- **Investment Minimum:** Not applicable (Government entity)
- **Risk Level:** N/A (Government function)
- **Required Licenses:** Not applicable to private entities
- **Mandatory Certifications:** Not applicable
- **Timeline to Operations:** N/A
- **Cost Estimates:** N/A

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
⚠️ **This is NOT available for private companies.** Bank Indonesia is Indonesia's central bank and operates under constitutional mandate. Private entities cannot engage in central banking activities.

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 64121 (Commercial Banks - Conventional)
- 64131 (Rural Banks - Conventional)

### KBLI 64121 - Bank Umum Konvensional (Conventional Commercial Bank)

**Indonesian:** Bank Umum Konvensional
**English:** Conventional Commercial Bank

#### Description
Conventional commercial banking business activities, including collecting funds from the public in the form of deposits and distributing them to the public in the form of credit and/or other forms, as well as conducting service activities in the payment system.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:** Maximum 40% (requires OJK approval for >40%; heavily restricted in practice)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **Core Capital: IDR 3 TRILLION** (US$200 million) - Mandatory by end 2022
  - Additional capital adequacy requirements apply (minimum CAR ratio)
- **Risk Level:** T (Tinggi/High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - OJK Banking License (primary approval)
  - Bank Indonesia operational approval
  - NIB from OSS system
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Fit & Proper Test for all Directors and Commissioners (OJK)
  - Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) minimum 8-12% depending on bank category
  - Risk-based capital requirements
  - Compliance with Basel III standards (implemented in Indonesia)
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **18-24 months minimum**
  - 3-6 months: Preparation and preliminary approval
  - 6-12 months: OJK licensing process (including fit & proper tests)
  - 3-6 months: Operational readiness and systems setup
  - Note: Timeline can extend significantly if documentation incomplete

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Core Capital Injection: IDR 3 trillion+ (mandatory)
  - Legal & Professional Fees: IDR 500 million - 1 billion
  - Banking Systems & Infrastructure: IDR 5-10 billion
  - Compliance & Risk Management Setup: IDR 2-5 billion
  - Office Setup & Initial Operations: IDR 1-3 billion
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 3.008 - 3.019 TRILLION** (US$200-205 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Underestimating capital requirements** - The IDR 3 trillion is MINIMUM core capital; operational capital needs are much higher
2. ⚠️ **Thinking 40% foreign ownership is automatic** - Any foreign ownership requires OJK approval and extensive documentation
3. ⚠️ **Failing fit & proper tests** - Directors/Commissioners need proven banking experience; this is not pro-forma
4. ⚠️ **Insufficient due diligence on shareholders** - OJK conducts deep background checks on ALL shareholders
5. ⚠️ **Inadequate technology infrastructure** - Banking systems must meet Bank Indonesia security and reporting standards
6. ⚠️ **Not budgeting for ongoing compliance costs** - Annual compliance/audit costs are IDR 500M - 1B+

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 64122 (Bank Umum Syariah - Islamic Commercial Bank)
- 64131 (BPR - Rural Bank Conventional)
- 64190 (Other Monetary Intermediation - includes fintech lending)

### KBLI 64122 - Bank Umum Syariah (Sharia Commercial Bank)

**Indonesian:** Bank Umum Syariah
**English:** Sharia/Islamic Commercial Bank

#### Description
Islamic commercial banking operations that comply with Sharia principles, collecting funds and distributing financing based on Islamic contracts (mudharabah, musyarakah, murabahah, etc.).

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:** Maximum 40% (same restrictions as conventional banks; requires OJK approval)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **Core Capital: IDR 3 TRILLION** (same as conventional banks)
  - Must comply with POJK 21 on Minimum Capital for Islamic Banks
- **Risk Level:** T (Tinggi/High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - OJK Banking License
  - Bank Indonesia operational approval
  - Sharia Compliance Certificate from Dewan Syariah Nasional (DSN-MUI)
  - NIB from OSS system
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Fit & Proper Test for Directors and Commissioners
  - **Sharia Supervisory Board (DPS)** appointment - MANDATORY
  - Sharia Compliance Review (ongoing)
  - Islamic finance qualifications for key personnel
  - Capital adequacy and Basel III compliance
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **20-26 months minimum**
  - Longer than conventional due to Sharia certification requirements
  - Additional 2-4 months for DPS approval and Sharia compliance framework

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Core Capital: IDR 3 trillion+ (mandatory)
  - Legal & Professional Fees: IDR 600 million - 1.2 billion (higher due to Sharia compliance)
  - Banking Systems (Sharia-compliant): IDR 6-12 billion
  - Sharia Compliance Framework: IDR 1-2 billion
  - DPS Honorarium & Training: IDR 500 million - 1 billion annually
  - Office & Operations: IDR 1-3 billion
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 3.0085 - 3.0192 TRILLION** (US$200-206 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Underestimating Sharia compliance complexity** - All products, contracts, and operations must be Sharia-compliant
2. ⚠️ **Not appointing qualified DPS members** - Dewan Syariah Nasional certification required
3. ⚠️ **Using conventional banking systems** - IT systems must support Islamic banking contracts
4. ⚠️ **Inadequate Islamic finance expertise** - Directors must demonstrate Sharia banking knowledge
5. ⚠️ **Mixing funds improperly** - Strict segregation requirements for Sharia funds

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- 64132 (BPRS - Rural Bank Sharia)

### KBLI 64131 - Bank Perkreditan Rakyat (BPR) - Conventional Rural Bank

**Indonesian:** Bank Perkreditan Rakyat (BPR)
**English:** Rural Bank (Conventional)

#### Description
Conventional banking business activities including collecting funds from the public in the form of deposits, savings, and/or similar forms and distributing them to the public in the form of credit. **Does NOT provide services in payment traffic** (no checking accounts, ATM cards, wire transfers, etc.).

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:** Maximum 40% (same as commercial banks; OJK approval required)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **Core Capital: IDR 6 BILLION** (effective December 31, 2024 per POJK 7/2024)
  - Previous requirement: IDR 2 billion
  - OJK mandated increase to strengthen BPR sector
- **Risk Level:** MT (Menengah Tinggi/Medium-High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - OJK BPR License
  - NIB from OSS system
  - Local government business permits
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Fit & Proper Test for Directors and Commissioners
  - Minimum Capital Adequacy Requirement (KPMM) - typically 8-12%
  - Annual audit by registered public accountant
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **12-18 months**
  - 2-3 months: Preparation and preliminary documents
  - 6-9 months: OJK licensing process
  - 3-6 months: Operational setup

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Core Capital: IDR 6 billion (mandatory minimum)
  - Legal & Professional Fees: IDR 100-300 million
  - Office & Branch Setup: IDR 500 million - 1 billion
  - IT Systems (basic): IDR 300-800 million
  - Initial Operating Capital: IDR 2-5 billion
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 8.9 - 13.1 BILLION** (US$600,000 - $900,000)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Confusing BPR with commercial banks** - BPRs CANNOT offer payment services (no checking accounts, cards, transfers)
2. ⚠️ **Starting with old IDR 2B capital** - As of Dec 2024, minimum is IDR 6B; many BPRs forced to merge
3. ⚠️ **Geographic restrictions overlooked** - BPRs typically limited to one province/region
4. ⚠️ **Inadequate credit risk management** - OJK heavily scrutinizes loan portfolio quality
5. ⚠️ **Not planning for capital adequacy** - Must maintain KPMM ratio; violations can trigger sanctions

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 64121 (Commercial Bank - Conventional)
- 64132 (BPRS - Rural Bank Sharia)

### KBLI 64132 - Bank Pembiayaan Rakyat Syariah (BPRS) - Sharia Rural Bank

**Indonesian:** Bank Pembiayaan Rakyat Syariah (BPRS)
**English:** Sharia Rural Bank

#### Description
Islamic rural banking operations that comply with Sharia principles. Like conventional BPR, BPRS does NOT provide payment traffic services, but uses Islamic financing contracts instead of interest-based loans.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:** Maximum 40% (OJK approval required)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **Core Capital: IDR 6 BILLION** (effective December 31, 2025 per POJK 7/2024)
  - Note: One year extension compared to conventional BPR
- **Risk Level:** MT (Menengah Tinggi/Medium-High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - OJK BPRS License
  - Sharia Compliance Certificate (DSN-MUI)
  - Dewan Pengawas Syariah (DPS) appointment
  - NIB from OSS system
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Fit & Proper Test for Directors and Commissioners
  - Islamic finance qualifications for management
  - DPS members must be DSN-MUI certified
  - Sharia Compliance Audit (annual)
  - Capital adequacy requirements
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **14-20 months**
  - Slightly longer than conventional BPR due to Sharia requirements

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Core Capital: IDR 6 billion (mandatory minimum)
  - Legal & Professional Fees: IDR 150-400 million
  - Sharia Compliance Setup: IDR 200-500 million
  - DPS Honorarium: IDR 100-300 million annually
  - Office & Operations: IDR 500 million - 1 billion
  - IT Systems (Sharia-compliant): IDR 400 million - 1 billion
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 7.35 - 9.2 BILLION** (US$500,000 - $620,000)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Not understanding Sharia financing products** - Must use murabahah, mudharabah, musyarakah, etc., not loans
2. ⚠️ **Inadequate DPS oversight** - DPS must review and approve all products and contracts
3. ⚠️ **Using conventional BPR systems** - Software must handle Sharia contract structures
4. ⚠️ **Missing deadlines for IDR 6B capital** - Deadline is Dec 2025; non-compliance may force closure/merger

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 64122 (Bank Umum Syariah)
- 64131 (BPR - Conventional)

### KBLI 64190 - Other Monetary Intermediation

**Indonesian:** Intermediasi Moneter Lainnya
**English:** Other Monetary Intermediation

#### Description
Other financial intermediation activities not elsewhere classified. **This includes fintech lending/P2P lending platforms** that provide credit but are not banks. Also covers specialized credit institutions.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - Maximum 85% for most P2P lending
  - Some subsectors may allow 100% (check OSS system)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **For P2P Lending: IDR 12.5 BILLION** (increased from IDR 2.5B)
  - POJK 40/2024 on IT-Based Co-Funding Services
  - For other intermediation: Minimum IDR 10 billion (general PT PMA requirement)
- **Risk Level:** MT-T (Medium-High to High Risk, depending on subsector)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - OJK License for P2P Lending (POJK 40/2024)
  - May require Bank Indonesia approval if involved in payment systems
  - NIB from OSS system
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - For P2P Lending: Regulatory Sandbox graduation (if new model)
  - Information security certification (ISO 27001 recommended)
  - Data protection compliance
  - Fit & proper test for management
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **For P2P Lending: 12-18 months**
  - 3-6 months: Regulatory sandbox (if applicable)
  - 6 months: OJK licensing process
  - 3-6 months: Platform development and compliance setup

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Minimum Equity: IDR 12.5 billion (for P2P lending)
  - Legal & Licensing Fees: IDR 200-500 million
  - Platform Development: IDR 2-5 billion
  - Compliance & Security Systems: IDR 1-3 billion
  - Initial Operations: IDR 1-2 billion
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 16.7 - 23 BILLION** (US$1.1 - 1.5 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Operating P2P lending without OJK license** - This is ILLEGAL; heavy fines and criminal prosecution
2. ⚠️ **Insufficient capital** - Many P2P platforms failed when OJK raised minimum to IDR 12.5B in 2024
3. ⚠️ **Poor data security** - Data breaches can result in license revocation
4. ⚠️ **Inadequate borrower protection** - OJK heavily scrutinizes interest rates and collection practices
5. ⚠️ **Not registering with AFPI** - Association membership may be required

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 64121 (Commercial Banks)
- 6492 (Other Credit Granting)
- 66411 (Payment Service Provider)

## DIVISION 642: ACTIVITIES OF HOLDING COMPANIES

### KBLI 64200 - Activities of Holding Companies

**Indonesian:** Aktivitas Perusahaan Holding
**English:** Activities of Holding Companies

#### Description
Activities of holding companies that control the assets of a group of subsidiary companies. Main activity is ownership/control of the group. **Holding companies do NOT participate in the business activities of their subsidiaries.** Includes services provided by counselors and negotiators in designing mergers and acquisitions.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - **100% allowed** for general holding companies (Perpres 10/2021)
  - Financial holding companies may have restrictions (check with OJK if subsidiaries are financial institutions)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - IDR 10 billion minimum investment (general PT PMA requirement)
  - Excludes land and buildings
- **Risk Level:** MR (Menengah Rendah/Medium-Low Risk) for general holdings
- **Required Licenses:**
  - NIB from OSS system
  - If financial holding company (controlling banks/insurance): OJK approval required
  - Articles of Association must clearly state holding company purpose
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - If financial holding company: Integrated financial conglomerate supervision by OJK
  - Consolidated financial reporting requirements
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **3-6 months** for non-financial holdings
  - **12-18 months** if financial holding company (OJK approval)

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Minimum Investment: IDR 10 billion (can be in subsidiary shares)
  - Legal & Incorporation Fees: IDR 30-100 million
  - Holding Structure Advisory: IDR 100-500 million
  - For Financial Holdings add: IDR 300 million - 1 billion (OJK compliance)
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 10.13 - 11.6 BILLION** (US$680,000 - $780,000)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Operating subsidiaries directly** - Holding companies should NOT conduct operational business
2. ⚠️ **Not understanding financial holding company rules** - If you control banks/insurance, OJK integrated supervision applies
3. ⚠️ **Transfer pricing violations** - Transactions between holding and subsidiaries must be at arm's length
4. ⚠️ **Inadequate capitalization of subsidiaries** - Each subsidiary must meet its own capital requirements
5. ⚠️ **Tax implications overlooked** - Dividend repatriation and consolidated tax obligations

#### Related KBLI Codes
- Various (depending on subsidiary operations)
- 64121 (if holding bank)
- 6511/6512 (if holding insurance companies)

## DIVISION 643: TRUSTS, FUNDS AND SIMILAR FINANCIAL ENTITIES

### KBLI 64300 - Trusts, Funds and Similar Financial Entities

**Indonesian:** Kepercayaan, Dana dan Badan Keuangan Serupa
**English:** Trusts, Funds and Similar Financial Entities

#### Description
Activities of entities that pool securities or other financial assets, without managing them, on behalf of shareholders or beneficiaries. Includes unit trusts, asset-backed securities arrangements, and similar financial entities.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - **100% allowed** (per Perpres 10/2021)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **IDR 2.5 BILLION** minimum paid-up capital
  - Total investment minimum IDR 10 billion (general PT PMA requirement)
- **Risk Level:** MT (Menengah Tinggi/Medium-High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - NIB from OSS system
  - May require OJK approval depending on specific trust structure
  - Custodian bank appointment (mandatory for asset pooling)
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Financial reporting audited by registered public accountant
  - Custodian agreement with licensed bank
  - Depending on assets: Securities registration
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **6-12 months**
  - 2-3 months: Company incorporation and initial approvals
  - 3-6 months: OJK coordination (if required)
  - 1-3 months: Custodian arrangements and operational setup

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Minimum Paid-Up Capital: IDR 2.5 billion
  - Legal & Professional Fees: IDR 100-300 million
  - Custodian Setup & Annual Fees: IDR 200-500 million
  - Compliance & Audit: IDR 100-300 million annually
  - Trust Documentation: IDR 50-200 million
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 2.95 - 3.8 BILLION** (US$200,000 - $255,000)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Confusing with fund management (KBLI 6630)** - This code is for passive pooling, NOT active management
2. ⚠️ **Not appointing licensed custodian** - Mandatory requirement; assets must be held by licensed bank
3. ⚠️ **Inadequate trust documentation** - Trust deed must clearly define beneficiary rights
4. ⚠️ **Failing to register securities** - If trust units are securities, OJK registration may be required
5. ⚠️ **Tax implications unclear** - Trust taxation complex; consult tax advisor

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 6630 (Fund Management Activities)
- 64121 (Commercial Bank - for custodian services)

## DIVISION 649: OTHER FINANCIAL SERVICE ACTIVITIES

### KBLI 64910 - Financial Leasing

**Indonesian:** Sewa Pembiayaan/Leasing
**English:** Financial Leasing (Finance Lease)

#### Description
Activities of leasing where the lessee assumes most of the risks and rewards of ownership. Financial leasing arrangements where the lessor transfers substantially all risks and rewards incidental to ownership; lease term covers most of the asset's economic life. Includes finance lease, capital lease, and leasing with purchase option.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - Maximum **85%** (per POJK 28/2014 on multifinance)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **Minimum Equity: IDR 100 BILLION** (increased per POJK 46/2024)
  - Previous: IDR 25 billion; OJK raised requirements significantly
- **Risk Level:** MT (Menengah Tinggi/Medium-High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - OJK Multifinance License (Perusahaan Pembiayaan)
  - NIB from OSS system
  - Must register with APPI (Indonesian Financing Association)
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Minimum equity maintenance (regulatory capital)
  - Fit & proper test for Directors and Commissioners
  - Annual financial audit by registered public accountant
  - Risk management framework
  - Good Corporate Governance (GCG) implementation
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **9-15 months**
  - 2-3 months: Company incorporation
  - 6-9 months: OJK licensing process
  - 1-3 months: Operational setup and systems

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Minimum Equity: IDR 100 billion (mandatory)
  - Legal & Licensing Fees: IDR 200-500 million
  - IT Systems (leasing software): IDR 500 million - 1.5 billion
  - Compliance Framework: IDR 300-800 million
  - Office & Initial Operations: IDR 500 million - 1 billion
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 101.5 - 103.8 BILLION** (US$6.8 - 7 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Confusing financial lease with operating lease** - Financial lease = ownership transfer; operating lease = KBLI 77xxx
2. ⚠️ **Insufficient capital** - Many small leasing companies forced to merge due to new IDR 100B requirement
3. ⚠️ **Inadequate asset valuation** - Leased assets must be properly valued and maintained
4. ⚠️ **Not distinguishing from banking** - Leasing companies CANNOT accept deposits
5. ⚠️ **Poor residual value management** - Must accurately estimate asset value at lease end

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 77310 (Rental and Leasing of Motor Vehicles - operating lease)
- 77390 (Rental of Other Machinery and Equipment - operating lease)
- 6492 (Other Credit Granting)

### KBLI 64920 - Other Credit Granting

**Indonesian:** Pemberian Kredit Lainnya
**English:** Other Credit Granting

#### Description
Credit granting activities not performed by monetary institutions. Includes consumer finance, credit card services, factoring, and other non-bank lending. **Does NOT include P2P lending (see 64190).**

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - Maximum **85%** (multifinance sector restriction)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **Minimum Equity: IDR 100 BILLION** (per POJK 46/2024)
  - Higher for infrastructure financing or certain specialized activities
- **Risk Level:** MT (Menengah Tinggi/Medium-High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - OJK Multifinance License
  - For specific activities (credit cards, factoring): Additional approvals
  - NIB from OSS system
  - APPI membership (Indonesian Financing Association)
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Capital adequacy maintenance
  - Fit & proper test for management
  - Risk management and GCG framework
  - For factoring: Maximum term limits (10 years with recourse, 2 years without)
  - Consumer protection compliance (interest rate caps, collection practices)
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **10-16 months**
  - 2-3 months: Incorporation
  - 6-10 months: OJK licensing (varies by activity type)
  - 2-3 months: Systems and operational setup

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Minimum Equity: IDR 100 billion
  - Legal & Licensing Fees: IDR 300-700 million
  - Credit Assessment Systems: IDR 1-3 billion
  - Collection & Risk Management: IDR 500 million - 1.5 billion
  - Initial Operations: IDR 1-2 billion
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 102.8 - 107.2 BILLION** (US$6.9 - 7.2 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Excessive interest rates** - OJK monitors and can sanction excessive rates
2. ⚠️ **Aggressive collection practices** - Consumer protection laws prohibit harassment
3. ⚠️ **Inadequate credit assessment** - Must have proper credit scoring and verification
4. ⚠️ **Not understanding factoring term limits** - Violating term restrictions can result in sanctions
5. ⚠️ **Operating without OJK license** - Illegal lending = criminal prosecution

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 64910 (Financial Leasing)
- 64190 (Other Monetary Intermediation - P2P lending)
- 64930 (Venture Capital)

### KBLI 64931 - Venture Capital Corporation (Conventional)

**Indonesian:** Perusahaan Modal Ventura Konvensional
**English:** Venture Capital Corporation (Conventional)

#### Description
Activities of venture capital companies that provide financing to high-risk, high-potential businesses through equity investments, convertible loans, or other financing instruments. Conventional (non-Sharia) venture capital.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - Maximum **85%** (per multifinance sector regulations)
  - Some venture capital may qualify for 100% if not classified as multifinance (check with OJK)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **Minimum Paid-Up Capital: IDR 50 BILLION** (per POJK 25/2023 and POJK 46/2024)
  - Previous requirement: IDR 25 billion
- **Risk Level:** MT (Menengah Tinggi/Medium-High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - OJK Venture Capital License (per POJK 25/2023)
  - NIB from OSS system
  - Must commence operations within **6 MONTHS** of license or face revocation
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Fit & proper test for Directors and Commissioners
  - Investment committee and governance structure
  - Portfolio valuation methodology (must be documented)
  - Annual financial audit
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **8-14 months**
  - 2-3 months: Incorporation (KBLI 64931 must be in Articles of Association)
  - 6-9 months: OJK licensing
  - Must start investing within 6 months post-license

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Minimum Paid-Up Capital: IDR 50 billion
  - Legal & Licensing Fees: IDR 200-500 million
  - Due Diligence & Valuation Systems: IDR 500 million - 1 billion
  - Investment Committee & Governance: IDR 200-500 million
  - Office & Operations: IDR 300-700 million
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 51.2 - 52.7 BILLION** (US$3.4 - 3.5 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Not starting operations within 6 months** - OJK can REVOKE license if no investments made
2. ⚠️ **Confusing with private equity** - VC focuses on early/growth stage; different regulations may apply
3. ⚠️ **Inadequate portfolio diversification** - OJK may scrutinize concentration risk
4. ⚠️ **Poor valuation methodology** - Portfolio must be marked-to-market regularly
5. ⚠️ **Not using KBLI 64931 in Articles** - Must explicitly state VC activities in corporate documents

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 64932 (Venture Capital - Sharia)
- 64920 (Other Credit Granting)

### KBLI 64932 - Venture Capital Corporation (Sharia)

**Indonesian:** Perusahaan Modal Ventura Syariah
**English:** Venture Capital Corporation (Sharia)

#### Description
Sharia-compliant venture capital that provides financing through Islamic investment structures (mudharabah, musyarakah) to early-stage and growth companies. All investments must comply with Sharia principles (no riba, gharar, maysir).

  - **ALL insurance agents must be licensed by OJK** - This is non-negotiable
  - Insurance broker company: OJK license
  - Individual agents: Certified Insurance Marketer (AAJI certification)
  - Brokers: Certified Insurance Broker
  - NIB from OSS system (for companies)
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - AAJI (Indonesian Life Insurance Association) certification for life agents
  - AAUI (Indonesian General Insurance Association) certification for general insurance agents
  - Insurance broker professional examination
  - Continuing education requirements (annual)
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **For individual agents: 1-3 months** (certification)
  - **For brokerage company: 6-10 months**
    - 2-3 months: Incorporation
    - 3-5 months: OJK licensing
    - 1-2 months: Operational setup

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - **For individual agents:**
    - Certification: IDR 5-15 million
    - Annual continuing education: IDR 2-5 million
  - **For insurance brokerage company:**
    - Capital: IDR 10 billion
    - Legal & Licensing: IDR 100-300 million
    - Agent Training & Certification: IDR 200-500 million
    - CRM & Systems: IDR 300-800 million
    - Office: IDR 300-700 million
    - **TOTAL: IDR 10.9 - 12.3 BILLION** (US$730,000 - $825,000)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Using unlicensed agents** - ALL agents must be OJK-licensed; violations can result in insurer sanctions
2. ⚠️ **Misrepresenting products** - Insurance marketing is heavily regulated; false claims = license revocation
3. ⚠️ **Not maintaining E&O insurance** - Professional liability insurance strongly recommended
4. ⚠️ **Confusing agent vs broker** - Agents represent insurers; brokers represent clients
5. ⚠️ **Not understanding commission regulations** - OJK may regulate commission structures
6. ⚠️ **Inadequate disclosure** - Must disclose all material information to clients

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 65110 (Life Insurance)
- 65120 (Non-Life Insurance)
- 66210 (Risk Evaluation)
- 66290 (Other Auxiliary to Insurance)

## DIVISION 663: FUND MANAGEMENT ACTIVITIES

### KBLI 66300 - Fund Management Activities

**Indonesian:** Aktivitas Manajemen Dana
**English:** Fund Management Activities

#### Description
Portfolio and fund management activities on a fee or contract basis. Includes mutual fund management (reksadana), hedge fund management, pension fund investment management, and other collective investment portfolio management. **Note:** Investment managers (Manajer Investasi) may use more specific KBLI **66311**.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - **100% allowed** (Perpres 10/2021)
  - No specific foreign ownership restrictions for investment managers
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **Minimum Paid-Up Capital: Currently IDR 25 BILLION** (existing requirement)
  - **Draft POJK proposes increase to IDR 50-100 BILLION** (not yet enacted as of 2024)
  - **Adjusted Net Working Capital (MKBD): Proposed IDR 20 BILLION** (up from IDR 200 million)
  - For Sharia Investment Managers: IDR 10 billion minimum
- **Risk Level:** MT (Menengah Tinggi/Medium-High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - **OJK Investment Manager License** (POJK 24/2014 and POJK 33/2024)
  - For KBLI: OSS only issues NIB; **licensing must come from OJK**
  - Custodian bank appointment - MANDATORY
  - For establishing DPLK (pension fund): Must have IDR 25 TRILLION average AUM over 3 years
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Fit & proper test for Directors and Commissioners
  - Investment Committee with qualified members
  - Wakil Manajer Investasi (WMI) license for investment representatives
  - Compliance with POJK 17/2022 on Investment Manager Code of Conduct
  - POJK 2/2023 on Good Corporate Governance for Investment Managers
  - Annual financial audit by registered public accountant
  - Each mutual fund must be registered with OJK before marketing
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **10-16 months**
  - 2-3 months: Company incorporation
  - 6-10 months: OJK licensing process (including fit & proper tests)
  - 1-2 months: Custodian arrangements
  - 1-2 months: First fund registration and launch preparation

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Minimum Paid-Up Capital: IDR 25 billion (current) to IDR 50-100 billion (proposed)
  - Legal & Licensing Fees: IDR 300-700 million
  - Custodian Arrangement: IDR 200-500 million setup; 0.05-0.2% of AUM annually
  - Portfolio Management Systems: IDR 1-3 billion
  - Compliance & Risk Management: IDR 500 million - 1.5 billion
  - Office & Operations: IDR 500 million - 1.5 billion
  - First Mutual Fund Setup: IDR 200-500 million
  - **TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: IDR 27.7 - 107.7 BILLION** (US$1.86 - 7.2 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Not anticipating capital requirement increases** - OJK planning to raise to IDR 50-100B; ensure long-term capitalization
2. ⚠️ **Launching funds before OJK registration** - Each fund must be registered; selling unregistered funds = illegal
3. ⚠️ **Not appointing custodian** - ALL fund assets must be held by licensed custodian bank
4. ⚠️ **Inadequate investment committee** - Must have qualified members with proven investment experience
5. ⚠️ **Poor performance disclosure** - Must disclose performance, fees, risks clearly to investors
6. ⚠️ **Violating investment restrictions** - Each fund type has specific investment limits (equity funds, money market, etc.)
7. ⚠️ **Not maintaining MKBD** - Adjusted net working capital must meet minimum; violations = sanctions
8. ⚠️ **Attempting DPLK without IDR 25T AUM** - Cannot establish pension fund without meeting this threshold

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 66311 (Investment Manager - more specific)
- 65300 (Pension Funding)
- 64300 (Trusts and Funds)
- 66120 (Securities Brokerage)

#### Description
More specific KBLI code for investment managers who manage securities portfolios for clients or manage collective investment portfolios for groups of clients. **Excludes insurance companies, pension funds, and banks conducting their own investments.**

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
**Same as KBLI 66300** - All warnings apply.

**Additional Note:**
- When registering with OSS, companies should use **KBLI 66311** if they are specifically investment managers (manajer investasi)

## DIVISION 664: PAYMENT SYSTEMS (NEW IN KBLI 2020)

### KBLI 66410 - Penyelenggara Sistem Pembayaran (Payment System Organizer)

**Indonesian:** Penyelenggara Sistem Pembayaran
**English:** Payment System Organizer

#### Description
New category in KBLI 2020 for payment system organizers. This division includes:

---

### KBLI 66411 - Penyedia Jasa Pembayaran (PJP) - Payment Service Provider

**Indonesian:** Penyedia Jasa Pembayaran (PJP)
**English:** Payment Service Provider

#### Description
Activities related to providing payment services to end users of the payment system on the front end. Includes: displaying funding source information; initiating transactions/acquiring (e-wallets, acquirer and payment gateway); issuing payment instruments/accounts; remittance/fund transfer services.

**Examples:** GoPay, OVO, DANA, PayPal (if licensed), and other e-wallet/payment providers.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - Varies by payment type
  - Generally **85% or 100%** depending on specific payment service (check Perpres 10/2021)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - Varies by payment service type
  - E-wallets: **IDR 10-25 billion** (check current Bank Indonesia regulations)
  - Payment gateways: **IDR 10 billion+**
- **Risk Level:** MT to T (Medium-High to High Risk)
- **Required Licenses:**
  - **Bank Indonesia License - MANDATORY** (not OJK; BI regulates payment systems)
  - PBI 23/7/2021 on Payment System Infrastructure Organizers
  - NIB from OSS system
  - Cybersecurity certification
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Fit & proper test for Directors and Commissioners (by Bank Indonesia)
  - Information security standards (ISO 27001 recommended)
  - Cybersecurity measures per BI Regulation 2/2024
  - Consumer protection compliance
  - Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorist Financing (CFT) compliance
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **12-18 months**
  - 2-3 months: Incorporation
  - 6-10 months: Bank Indonesia licensing
  - 3-5 months: System development and security certification

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Capital: IDR 10-25 billion (varies by service)
  - Legal & Licensing: IDR 300-800 million
  - Payment Platform Development: IDR 3-10 billion
  - Cybersecurity & Compliance: IDR 1-3 billion
  - Bank Indonesia Compliance: IDR 500 million - 1.5 billion
  - Operations & Marketing: IDR 1-3 billion
  - **TOTAL: IDR 15.8 - 43.3 BILLION** (US$1.06 - 2.9 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **Confusing OJK with Bank Indonesia** - Payment systems are regulated by **Bank Indonesia**, not OJK
2. ⚠️ **Inadequate cybersecurity** - PBI 2/2024 mandates strict cybersecurity measures; violations = license suspension
3. ⚠️ **Poor fund segregation** - Customer funds must be properly segregated and protected
4. ⚠️ **Not understanding e-money vs e-wallet regulations** - Different payment types have different requirements
5. ⚠️ **Operating without Bank Indonesia license** - Illegal payment services = criminal prosecution
6. ⚠️ **Inadequate AML/CFT controls** - Payment providers are high-risk for money laundering

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 66412 (Payment System Infrastructure Organizer)
- 66413 (Payment System Support Organizer)
- 64190 (Other Monetary Intermediation - P2P lending often integrates payments)

### KBLI 66412 - Penyelenggara Infrastruktur Sistem Pembayaran (PIP)

**Indonesian:** Penyelenggara Infrastruktur Sistem Pembayaran (PIP)
**English:** Payment System Infrastructure Organizer

#### Description
Activities related to organizing infrastructure that supports payment service providers and/or payment system participants in processing payment transactions. This includes clearing houses, switching systems, and other payment infrastructure.

**Examples:** National Payment Gateway, ATM network switches, clearing houses.

#### Regulatory Details
- **Foreign Ownership:**
  - **Highly restricted** - Payment infrastructure is considered strategic
  - Likely 0-49% maximum (verify with Bank Indonesia)
- **Investment Minimum:**
  - **IDR 50 BILLION+** (estimated; infrastructure requires significant capital)
- **Risk Level:** T (Tinggi/High Risk) - systemic importance
- **Required Licenses:**
  - **Bank Indonesia License** per PBI 23/7/2021
  - Multiple approvals due to systemic importance
  - NIB from OSS system
- **Mandatory Certifications:**
  - Extensive cybersecurity requirements
  - Disaster recovery and business continuity systems
  - Real-time monitoring and surveillance
  - 99.9%+ uptime requirements
  - Regular penetration testing and security audits
- **Timeline to Operations:**
  - **18-36 months**
  - Payment infrastructure is complex and requires extensive testing

- **Cost Estimates:**
  - Capital: IDR 50 billion+
  - Infrastructure Development: IDR 20-100 billion
  - Cybersecurity: IDR 5-15 billion
  - **TOTAL: IDR 75 - 165 BILLION** (US$5 - 11 million)

#### Common Mistakes & Warnings
1. ⚠️ **This is HIGHLY restricted** - Payment infrastructure is strategic; difficult for private entities
2. ⚠️ **Underestimating complexity** - Payment infrastructure requires extensive technical expertise
3. ⚠️ **Inadequate redundancy** - System failures affect entire payment ecosystem; must have backup systems

#### Related KBLI Codes
- 66411 (Payment Service Provider)
- 66413 (Payment System Support)

## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS FOR CATEGORY K (FINANCIAL & INSURANCE ACTIVITIES)

### 1. CAPITAL IS KING
- **Banking:** IDR 6 billion (BPR) to IDR 3 TRILLION (commercial bank)
- **Insurance:** IDR 250 billion to IDR 1 trillion (by 2028)
- **Reinsurance:** IDR 500 billion to IDR 2 trillion
- **Securities/Investment Management:** IDR 25-100 billion
- **Multifinance:** IDR 100 billion
- **Venture Capital:** IDR 50 billion

**You must have DEEP pockets. If you need to ask if you can afford it, you probably cannot.**

---

### 2. OJK/BANK INDONESIA APPROVAL IS NON-NEGOTIABLE
- **Timeline:** 6-12 months for licensing (can extend to 18-24 months)
- **Fit & Proper Tests:** Directors, Commissioners, and key shareholders undergo extensive background checks
- **No shortcuts:** Connections, bribes, or workarounds will get your application rejected or worse

---

### 3. FOREIGN OWNERSHIP IS HEAVILY RESTRICTED
- **Banking:** 40% maximum (requires OJK approval)
- **Insurance/Reinsurance:** 80% maximum
- **Multifinance/Securities:** 85% maximum (99% for registered foreign securities firms)
- **Some subsectors:** 100% allowed (fund management, some fintech)

**Plan your ownership structure carefully BEFORE applying.**

---

### 4. EXPECT 12-24 MONTHS TO LAUNCH
- **Banking:** 18-24 months
- **Insurance:** 14-20 months
- **Securities:** 12-18 months
- **Payment Systems:** 12-18 months
- **Investment Managers:** 10-16 months

**This is NOT a quick venture. Budget for extended runway.**

---

### 5. ONGOING COMPLIANCE COSTS ARE MASSIVE
- **Annual Audits:** IDR 200 million - 1 billion+
- **Regulatory Reporting:** Continuous
- **Actuaries (insurance/pension):** IDR 1-3 billion annually
- **Cybersecurity (payment systems):** IDR 1-3 billion annually
- **Risk Management:** IDR 500 million - 2 billion annually

**Your first-year costs are just the beginning.**

---

### 6. PROFESSIONAL EXPERTISE IS MANDATORY
You will need:
- **Indonesian legal counsel** specializing in financial services (IDR 500M - 2B in fees)
- **Big 4 accounting firm** (for audit and advisory)
- **Actuaries** (for insurance/pension)
- **Compliance consultants**
- **Technology partners** (for banking/payment systems)

**Do NOT attempt DIY. This sector requires experts.**

---

### 7. COMMON REASONS FOR REJECTION
1. **Insufficient capital** (not just minimum, but operational capital too)
2. **Shareholders with questionable backgrounds** (OJK conducts deep due diligence)
3. **Directors/Commissioners failing fit & proper tests** (lack of relevant experience)
4. **Unclear business plan** (must demonstrate viability and market need)
5. **Using loans for capital injection** (prohibited for insurance and securities)
6. **Inadequate risk management framework**
7. **Poor corporate governance structure**

---

### 8. SECTOR-SPECIFIC WARNINGS

#### BANKING
- Only consider if you have IDR 3 TRILLION+ in committed capital
- Expect intense scrutiny from OJK and Bank Indonesia
- Must comply with Basel III requirements
- BPR option (IDR 6B) is more accessible but limited to one region and no payment services

#### INSURANCE
- Must appoint qualified in-house actuary (30 companies sanctioned in 2023 for non-compliance)
- Capital requirements increasing in phases through 2028
- Reinsurance capital requirements are 2X higher than primary insurance
- 80% foreign ownership cap is firm

#### SECURITIES & INVESTMENT MANAGEMENT
- Cannot use loans for capital injection (OJK prohibits)
- Net adjusted capital must be maintained at all times
- IDX membership required for securities brokerage
- Investment managers: Capital requirements likely increasing to IDR 50-100B

#### MULTIFINANCE (LEASING, CONSUMER FINANCE)
- Minimum equity jumped to IDR 100 billion in 2024 (POJK 46/2024)
- Many small players forced to merge or exit
- 85% foreign ownership cap

#### FINTECH & PAYMENT SYSTEMS
- P2P Lending: Minimum equity IDR 12.5 billion (increased from IDR 2.5B; many platforms exited)
- Payment systems: Regulated by **Bank Indonesia** (NOT OJK)
- Cybersecurity requirements extremely strict (PBI 2/2024)

#### VENTURE CAPITAL
- Must commence operations within 6 MONTHS of license or face revocation
- Minimum capital IDR 50 billion
- Must use KBLI 64931 (conventional) or 64932 (Sharia) in Articles of Association
